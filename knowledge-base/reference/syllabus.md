## Knowledge Level Key

| Level | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **D** — Detailed knowledge | A thorough knowledge and substantial understanding, permitting detailed comment and the solution of complex problems. |
| **G** — General knowledge | Knowledge and understanding sufficient for intelligent comment and the solution of straightforward problems. |
| **O** — Overview knowledge | An awareness of principles or rules, sufficient for the student to know when they should refer to an outside source and why. Candidates with such knowledge should be able to articulate issues which would require further study and to discuss briefly the likely result of that enquiry. |

## Aim

The aim of the examination is to test candidates' knowledge and understanding of the legal, regulatory and ethical framework of the UK financial services sector and their ability to apply such knowledge and understanding in a practical manner. Candidates will also be required to display an awareness of current topics of interest in the field and be able to debate the major principles of such topics.

The exam is predominantly focused on the laws, rules and requirements that apply in the UK, but candidates should be aware of certain international rules and regulations that can impact the UK due to their extraterritorial application and scope.

**The regulatory environment is subject to significant change and candidates should ensure that their knowledge is up to date. Therefore, you should read the financial press and material published by the UK regulators (HM Treasury, the FCA, the PRA and the Bank of England) and other agencies whose actions affect the UK, and global financial services to the extent that they may impact the UK.**

## Examination Structure

A three-hour-and-fifteen-minute examination, with the paper divided into three sections:

- **Section A** – up to ten compulsory short answer questions. This section will carry 30% of the marks.

- **Section B** – a choice of four practical, problem-solving questions from which students are required to answer two. **ONE QUESTION WILL BE COMPULSORY.** This section will carry 40% of the marks, and these will be awarded to reflect not only technical knowledge, but the ability to apply that knowledge to the problem and to deal practically and effectively with the situation presented, **INCLUDING AN ETHICAL AND PROFESSIONAL RESPONSE TO THE SITUATION, BASED UPON THE CISI CODE OF CONDUCT, WHERE APPROPRIATE**.

- **Section C** – a choice of four essay questions from which students are required to answer two. This section will carry 30% of the marks and these will be awarded to reflect both knowledge and understanding, together with clarity of arguments presented.

Candidates are required to keep their knowledge up to date with regard to changes in the regulatory environment.

## Part 5 – Current Regulatory Developments

### Objectives

Candidates are required to keep up to date with UK and international developments that could have an impact on the UK financial services sector.

Candidates are encouraged to refer to the Financial Services and Markets Act (FSMA) 2000, the FCA Handbook and the PRA Rulebook.

- FSMA – www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2000/8/contents

- FCA – www.fca.org.uk

- PRA – www.bankofengland.co.uk/pra

Candidates are also advised to familiarise themselves with the HM Treasury, FCA, PRA and Bank of England websites, including:

- dear CEO letters (FCA only)

- market watch newsletters (FCA only)

- regulatory decisions (FCA and PRA)

- published speeches (FCA, PRA and HM Treasury)

- consultation papers, discussion papers, feedback statements, policy statements and supervisory statements (HM Treasury, Bank of England, FCA and PRA)

- proposals and call for evidence (HM Treasury, FCA)

- annual report/annual business plan (FCA and PRA), and

- thematic reviews (FCA only).

In addition, to a lesser degree you may also wish to monitor the websites of the European Commission, ESMA, EBA and the EIOPA.
