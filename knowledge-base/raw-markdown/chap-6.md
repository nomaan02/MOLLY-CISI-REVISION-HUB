## **Risk** 

## **(6.1.1) Management of risk** 

- In business, as in life, if you do not take any risks, you are unlikely to get any reward (business profits) 

   - The goal of business is **not** to **avoid** risks, but to **manage** them so as to keep them at a reasonable (and manageable) level, consistent with the firm’s business objectives. 

   - One of the roles of compliance is to **help with the management of the risks** to which the firm is exposed, in order to achieve the firm’s business objectives. 

- Key components of a simple risk management framework 

   - Risk policies and governance at board level 

   - Risk oversight function to 

      - Identify of risk 

      - Assess of risk 

      - Ensure the control of risk 

      - Monitor and report on risks and controls 

      - Day-to-day risk management

## **Conduct Risk** 

## **Conduct Risk** 

- FCA expects firms to develop their own definition, and how it applies to them 

   - My definition – ‘The risk that firm behaviour will result in poor outcomes for customers’ 

   - Usually as a result of a culture of short-term profit maximisation at the expense of fair treatment of customers, or ethical behaviour 

## • Firms should **promote** : 

- Good behaviour across all aspects of the organisation 

- A culture of zero tolerance for misconduct 

## • Firms should **refrain from** : 

- Prioritising profits over ethics, or commercial interests over customer interests 

- A ‘tick-box-based’, or overly-legalistic, approach to compliance 

- The idea of ‘caveat emptor’, or that disclosure absolves the firm from all 

   - responsibilities to the customer 

- Complying only with the letter, not the spirit, of laws and regulations

## **Financial Crime Risk Management** 

- **(3.4.7) The FCA Financial Crime Guide** 

- The FCA’s ‘Financial Crime: A Firms Guide to countering financial crime risks’ provides practical assistance and information for firms of all types and sizes 

- Firms have flexibility in how to comply with financial crime obligations as long as the desired results are achieved 

   - Firms should take a risk-based, outcomes-focused approach 

- The guide gives good and poor examples of: 

   - Governance 

   - Structure 

   - Risk assessment 

   - Policies and procedures 

   - Recruitment, vetting, training, awareness and remuneration 

   - Quality of oversight

## **An Independent, Resourced Compliance Function** 

## **(6.1.3) Independence of the compliance function** 

## • Independence means: 

- Formal status 

- Head of compliance 

- Avoid conflicts 

- Access to information and personnel 

## • The Three Lines of Defence model 

- First line of defence – business operations 

- Second line of defence – accounts, risk management and compliance functions 

- Third line of defence – internal audit 

## **(6.1.3) Adequate resources** 

- Sufficient and proportionate to ensure compliance risk is effectively managed 

- Compliance staff should have: 

   - Necessary qualification, experience and professional and personal qualities 

   - Sound understanding of compliance laws, rules and standards

## **An Effective, Compliance Function** 

## **(6.1.3) An effective, compliance function** 

- Key purposes of a compliance function 

   - Assurance to senior managers and board of compliance 

   - Advise board, management and staff of compliance responsibilities 

   - Identify and record good and bad practice 

   - Provide analysis and early warning of regulatory change 

   - Build a relationship with regulators 

   - Respond and co-ordinate responses to proposed legislation and regulation 

- Characteristics of an effective compliance function 

   - Seen as an advantage to the business 

   - Consulted regularly 

   - Valued by the staff

## **CISI Code of Conduct** 

## **(6.2.4) CISI Values can help to decide the best course of action** 

- Is the **course of action** I am considering: 

   - **Clear** _**(Honest)**_ **?** Have I been truthful about my action or decision with everyone involved, and told no lies or ‘half-truths’? 

   - **Impartial** _**(Open)**_ **?** Is everyone affected by my action or decision aware of the consequences? 

   - **Straightforward** _**(Transparent)**_ **?** Have I made sure that my action or decision will not result in any party being unknowingly disadvantaged or unduly advantaged? 

   - **Informed** _**(Fair)**_ **?** Have I considered the interests of my potential stakeholders and not been misleading when making my decision? 

- Professional integrity requires **prioritisation** 

   - **Client first** 

   - **Firm second** 

   - **Self third**
