## **Trading On-exchange or Over-the-counter: Summary** 

## **Trading on-exchange vs off-exchange (‘over-the-counter’)** 

||**Over-the-counter (OTC)**|**Trading on exchange**|
|---|---|---|
|**Contract terms**|•<br>Bespoke: tailored to meet the needs of<br>the investor|•<br>Contract specifications standardised by the<br>exchange|
|**Liquidity**|•<br>Can be limited leading to slower<br>execution|•<br>Excellent on major contracts|
|**Margin**|•<br>Normally no margin|•<br>Margin normally required|
|**Regulation**|•<br>Less stringent, but increasing regulation<br>of products (e.g. via EMIR)<br>•<br>More stringent restrictions on who may<br>invest in them|•<br>Exchanges subject to significant regulation|
|**Counterparty risk**|•<br>Exposed to default risk|•<br>No member default risk due to clearing<br>house|
|**Reporting**|•<br>Confidentiality|•<br>Market transparency|
|**Price**|•<br>Negotiated or  ‘request for quote’ (RFQ)|•<br>Best execution|
|**Hedging**|•<br>Specific hedging requirements can be<br>met|•<br>Hedges based on standardised contracts<br>need to be actively managed|

## **Multilateral Trading Facilities (MTFs)** 

## **(4.3.1) FCA Handbook requirements of MTFs** 

- MTFs regulated by MAR 5 

   - Transparent and non-discretionary rules for fair and orderly trading 

   - Objective criteria for the efficient execution of orders 

   - Member firms to be fit and proper (and have appropriate levels of competence, organisation and resources) 

   - Sufficient publicly available information to enable users (investors) to form a valid judgment 

   - Having efficient settlement systems 

   - Firms to monitor compliance by its users with the rules 

   - Problems (e.g. rule breaches, market abuse, etc.) to be reported to FCA 

   - Similar pre- and post-trade transparency requirements to exchanges (i.e. prices at which deals are advertised and transacted) 

- OTFs regulated in a similar way by MAR 5A

## **Systematic Internalisers (SIs)** 

- **(4.5.1) Systematic internalisers (SIs)** 

- ‘An investment firm which, on an organised, frequent and systematic basis, deals on own account by executing client orders outside a regulated market or an MTF or OTF’ 

   - Effectively, this covers any firm which regularly deals as principal with its own clients – other than as a market maker on an exchange 

## **FCA Handbook requirements of SIs** 

- SIs regulated by MAR 6 

   - Pre-trade transparency requirements 

   - Execution of orders at fair prices 

   - Standards and conditions for trading 

- Firm must notify the FCA when it becomes/ceases to be an SI 

   - MiFID requires competent authorities to publish, and review annually, a list of all SIs which they have authorised as investment firms
