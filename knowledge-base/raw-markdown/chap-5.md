## **FCA Strategy** 

## **(2.1.5 / 5.1.1) FCA Strategy 2024-25** 

- FCA Business Plan 2024-5 focuses on three areas 

   - Commitment 1: Reducing and preventing financial crime 

   - Commitment 2: Putting consumers’ needs first 

   - Commitment 3: Strengthening the UK’s position in global financial markets 

## • FCA Market Effectiveness Review 

- Continuing evolution of Listing Rules to make them more efficient for companies issuing securities

## **Other forthcoming UK regulatory reforms** 

## **(5.1.2) Wholesale Markets Review** 

- Determining how much retained EU law needs adapting 

   - Recalibrating regulations to suit UK’s position as a global capital marketplace 

   - Upholding regulatory standards and economic growth 

## **(5.1.3) UK Regulatory Reforms** 

## • Financial Services and Markets Act 2023 

- Ensuring UK financial services regulatory framework is ‘fit for purpose’ outside the EU 

- A greater focus on growth and competitiveness for the regulators 

   - Including a new secondary objective around boosting UK economy’s international competitiveness 

## • Edinburgh Reforms 

- Further attempts to capitalise on post-Brexit freedoms, e.g. by reviewing ringfencing regime for banks

# **Current and Future Regulatory Developments: EU** 

Knowledge | Skills | Conduct 

## **Other forthcoming EU regulatory reforms** 

## **(5.2.1-4) Various EU regulations, taxes and directives** 

- Review of Central Securities Depositories Regulation (CSDR) 

   - Aims to improve the securities settlement process across Europe 

   - Introduces a common authorisation, supervision and regulatory framework for CSDs 

- European Markets Infrastructure Regulation (EMIR III) 

   - Response to risks caused by historic lack of regulation of OTC derivatives markets 

- Changes to MiFID II and MiFIR 

   - Aimed at improving regulation of wholesale markets 

   - Various reforms that will enhance power of European Commission and ESMA, and cause further divergence with UK MiFID regime 

- ESG and the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) 

   - Improving corporate reporting of ESG matters
