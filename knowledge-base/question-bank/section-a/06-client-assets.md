- **A6.1** Describe the main requirements that apply to a firm becoming authorised to provide custody services. _(3 marks)_ 

Custody is provided for under CASS. The custody rules generally require that assets are held safely and securely. 

General rules apply with regard to the following: 

   - a) segregation of safe custody investment from designated investments; 

   - b) registration and recording title in the name of the client, a nominee company, a custodian, the firm or another person subject to the specified rules; 

   - c) firms must hold documents of title to safe custody investments either in the possession of the firm or custodian, the firm or another person subject to specified rules; 

   - d) relevant terms and conditions must be set out in an appropriate client agreement; 

   - e) appropriate risk disclosures must be made; 

   - f) comply with restrictions applicable with regard to stock lending; 

   - g) appropriate reconciliations must be made. 

- **A6.2** The FCA’s client money and assets rules require that client money is segregated from a firm’s assets. Provide three exceptions to this general rule. _(3 marks)_ 

Segregation exceptions include: 

- a) Opt outs by professional clients (for non-MiFID business) or eligible counterparties subject to appropriate disclosure and documentation; 

- b) Money due and payable to the firm (such as fees and commissions); 

- c) Money from an affiliated company; 

- d) Money paid in connection with a delivery versus payment transaction (DVP); and 

- e) Money held by an approved bank on behalf of a client in an account with itself. 


**A6.3** Explain the purpose of the ‘client money’ rules. _(2 marks)_ 

Principle 10 (clients’ assets) requires a firm to arrange adequate protection for clients’ assets when the firm is responsible for them. An essential part of that protection is the proper accounting and handling of client money The rules are designed to restrict the comingling of clients’ and firms’ money, to minimise the risk of clients’ money being treated as the firm’s assets in the event of the insolvency of the firm and to minimise the risk of clients’ money being used without the consent of or against the wishes of the clients. 

- **A6.4** Briefly explain the parts of the CASS sourcebook within the FCA's Handbook of Rules and Guidance. _(3 marks)_ 

CASS is the Client Assets sourcebook which contains the requirements concerning the holding of client assets and client money in the FCA Handbook of Rules and Guidance. This includes specific provision with regard to Collateral (CASS 3), Client money: insurance mediation activity (CASS 5), Custody rules (CASS 6), Client money rules (CASS 7), Client money distribution (CASS 7A) and Mandates (CASS 8). 

- **A6.5** Explain the general purpose and content of the CASS sourcebook within the FCA's Handbook. _(3 marks)_ 

CASS is the Client Assets sourcebook which contains the requirements concerning the holding of client assets and client money in the FCA Handbook of Rules and Guidance. This includes specific provision with regard to Application and Classification (CASS 1 & 1A), Collateral (CASS 3), Client Money: Insurance Mediation Activity (CASS 5), Custody Rules (CASS 6), Client Money Rules (CASS 7), Client Money distribution (CASS 7A), Mandates (CASS 8), Information to Clients (CASS 9), Cass Resolution Pack (CASS 110), Debt Management Client Money (CASS 11), Commodities Futures Trading Commission Part 30 Exemption Order (CASS 12) and Claims Management: Client Money (CASS 13).
