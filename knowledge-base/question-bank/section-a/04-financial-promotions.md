**A4.1** Explain the difference between a “real time” and a “non-real time” financial promotion. _(3 marks)_ 

The meaning of “real time” and “non-real time” is set out in Article 7 of the Financial Promotion Order. 

Real time generally refers to instantaneous or immediate forms of financial promotion involving direct contact between the customer and a regulated firm. This will generally include personal meetings as well as telephone or other interactive forms of communication. 

Non-real time refers to more static forms of advertisement not involving any immediate contact between the potential customer and the firm. This will generally include mail shots as well as magazine and TV advertising as well as internet promotions to the extent that no direct communication is involved. 

Relevant factors to be taken into account in determining whether a communication is nonreal time include: 

   - a) More than one person is involved using a standard format; 

   - b) The communication creates a record; 

   - c) It does not require any immediate response. 

- **A4.2** The Financial Promotion Rules on the communication of unsolicited real time financial promotions (cold calls) contain a number of requirements that must be complied with. List three of these requirements. _(3 marks)_ 

Cold calls may only be made in the following cases: 

- a) The communication falls within one of the exemptions set out in the  Financial Promotions Order (such as certified sophisticated investor); 

- b) The communication falls within one of the COBS exceptions (such as eligible counterparty or professional client); 

- c) Existing retail client and the relationship has been agreed; 

- d) Certain marketable packaged products (such as regulated non-derivative collective investment schemes); 

- e) Services relating to readily realisable securities other than warrants. 


- **A4.3** Identify six exceptions under the Financial Promotions Order for breach of the restriction on the issuance of financial promotions under s 21 of the FSMA. _(3 marks)_ 

The main exceptions contained in the FPO include: 

   - a) Generic promotions; 

   - b) Investment professionals; 

   - c) Overseas recipients; 

   - d) Deposits and insurance; 

   - e) One-off communications; 

   - f) Certified high net worth individuals (net income of over £100,000 or net assets of over £250,000); 

   - g) Certified sophisticated investors; 

   - h) High net worth companies. 

- **A4.4** Identify and explain the main regulatory requirements that apply with regard to financial promotions under the FSMA 2000 and the FCA Handbook and the source of the main exemptions available. _(3 marks)_ 

The main obligation on financial promotion is imposed under s21 (1) and (2) of the FSMA. This requires that a financial promotion must only be communicated by an approved person or have been approved by an authorised person. 

This is supported by the exemptions that may be issued under either the Financial Promotions Order (issued by the Treasury) or set out by the FCA in the COBS (Conduct of Business Sourcebook) within the FCA Handbook. 

- **A4.5** Give three examples of financial promotions to which the FCA’s Conduct of Business (COBS) Rules do not apply. _(3 marks)_ 

The Financial Promotions Order provides a number of exemptions, for example, for communications directed at investment professionals, to certified high net worth individuals and certified sophisticated investors. Also excluded are a company’s statutory accounts and 


directors’ reports, and disclosures/announcements required by the listing rules or the rules of an exchange or market. 

The COBS Rules do not apply to communications to a person outside the UK made from a place outside the UK. 

**A4.6** Describe the regime relating to financial promotions. 

## _(4 marks)_ 

A financial promotion is an invitation or inducement to engage in investment activity. Under section 21 of FSMA 2000 it is an offence in the course of business to communicate an invitation or inducement to engage in investment activity unless either the person doing so is an authorised person or the content of the financial promotion is approved by an authorised person 

The Financial Promotions Order sets out a number of exemptions from section 21 for example for promotions directed at authorised persons or certified high net worth or sophisticated investors. There are also exemptions for promotions required by statute such as a company’s annual report or by the rules of an exchange or market. 

Where a firm issues or approves the content of a financial promotion then it must ensure the person who does so is competent to perform that role and confirms that the promotion complies with the requirements of the rules in COBS 4. Like all communications with clients a financial promotion must be fair, clear and not misleading. 

**A4.7** Give four examples of exempt financial promotions. 

## _(2 marks)_ 

The Financial Promotions Order specifies certain exemptions from s21 as follows: 

Communications directed at: 

- Investment professionals 

- Certified high net worth individuals and associations of high net worth individuals 


Also the following: 

- Company accounts 

- Directors’ reports 

- Listing particulars and prospectuses 

- Promotions by overseas persons to overseas persons 

- One off non-real time financial promotions and one-off solicited real time promotions 

- A financial promotion which contains only one or more of the following, name of the firm, name of an investment, a contact point, a logo, a brief factual description of the firm’s activities, fees or investment products, the price or yields of investments and the charges. 

- A personal quotation or illustration form 

- A financial promotion subject to the Takeover Code 

- A financial promotion in the form of a decision tree for a stakeholder pension scheme 

**A4.8** What is meant by a solicited financial promotion? 

## _(2 marks)_ 

Where contact is initiated by the customer or the financial promotion takes place in response to an express request from the recipient, then it is described as solicited: if it is initiated by the firm, then it is unsolicited. A person is not treated as having expressly requested a call, visit or dialogue because he either omits to indicate that he does not wish to receive such contact or because he agrees to standard terms that state such visits etc. will take place. Therefore a customer must positively indicate that he wants such contact or specifically request it. 

- **A4.9** In what circumstances may a firm make an unsolicited real time financial promotion (“cold call”)? _(2 marks)_ 

A firm cannot make a cold call unless: 

- a) the recipient is an existing customer and envisages receiving cold calls; or 


   - b) the financial promotion relates to a generally marketable packaged product (which is not a higher volatility fund); or 

   - c) the financial promotion relates to readily realisable securities other than warrants). 

- **A4.10** What rules apply when a firm communicates to clients, or potential clients, information which contains an indication of the past performance of an investment? 

_(3 marks)_ 

Where past performance is indicated, the following rules apply: 

   - That indication must not be the most prominent feature of the communication. 

   - Performance information must cover at least the last five years (or the whole available period, if less), and must be based on complete 12 month periods. 

   - The reference period and source of information must be stated. 

   - There must be a prominent warning that the figures refer to the past and that past performance is not a reliable indicator of future results. 

   - The currency must be clearly stated (if different from that of the EEA state where the retail client is resident), together with a warning about the effect of currency fluctuations. 

   - If the indication is based on gross performance, the effect of commissions, fees or charges must be disclosed. 

- **A4.11** Specify the consequences of breach of the restriction on financial promotion imposed under section 21 of the FSMA. _(2 marks)_ 

The consequences of breach of the restriction on the issuance of unauthorised or unapproved financial promotions are as follows: 

- a) the person is guilty of an offence and liable for up to six months imprisonment (on summary conviction) or two years (on indictment) or a fine or both (s 25(1)); 

- b) agreements entered into in consequence of an unlawful communication are unenforceable against the other party who is also entitled to recover any money or property paid and compensation for loss sustained (s30(2)(a) and (b)); 

- c) a public or private censure may be imposed (s 205); 

- d) a general financial penalty may be imposed (s 206(1)); 


   - e) an action for damages may possibly be available (s 138D); 

   - f) an injunction may be issued (s 380(1)); 

   - g) the FCA may apply for a restitution order (s382(1)). 

- **A4.12** List six of the general requirements that apply with regard to the publication of past performance information under COBS within the FCA Handbook. _(3 marks)_ 

The FCA requirements with regard to past performance information under COBS 4.6 include the following: 

- a) information must not be the most prominent feature; 

- b) information should cover, at least, the five preceding years; 

- c) information should cover 12 month periods; 

- d) information should state the reference period and source; 

- e) a prominent warning should be included against reliance on past information; 

- f) specify the relevant currency and warn that currencies may fluctuate; 

- g) disclose the effect of commissions, fees and charges on gross performance. 

**A4.13** Explain the general requirement imposed under s21 Financial Services and Markets Act (FSMA) on financial promotions. _(3 marks)_ 

As 21 FSMA requires that a financial promotion, in the form of an invitation or inducement to engage in an investment activity, must be issued by an authorised person or the content approved by an authorised person (s 21(1) and (2)). All financial promotions must be clear, fair and misleading (COBS 4.2). This is subject to the exemptions provided for under the Financial Promotion Order (FPO) as amended. 

The restrictions on financial promotions do not apply to purely factual communications nor to eligible counterparties with regard to MiFID business nor to incoming MiFID communications with these regulated in the home EEA state. 

A person who breaches the restrictions on financial promotions is guilty of an offence and liable for up to two years imprisonment (six months on summary conviction) or a fine or both (s 25(1)) subject to a reasonable belief or reasonable precautions defence ((s 25(2)).
