## 3. Approved Persons Regime (APER)

The Approved Persons Regime (APER) only applies to a limited number of regulated firms, which mainly includes appointed representatives.

The Approved Person Regime no longer applies to PRA regulated firms – all PRA regulated firms are now subject to the SM&CR.

FSMA refers to 'authorised persons' and 'approved persons'. For authorisation purposes, the term 'person' refers to legal persons rather than individual human beings. A limited company, for example, is a legal person.

- Authorised persons, generally, authorisation, as discussed in chapter 1, section 1.3, is required under s19 of FSMA for firms or businesses that are carrying out regulated activities by way of business in the UK. In order to obtain authorisation, firms must satisfy the threshold conditions (see section 8).

- Approved persons, under s59, individual people who work for such authorised persons (ie, authorised firms) as employees or appointed representatives must be approved if carrying out what are referred to as controlled functions.

An individual is only permitted to perform a controlled function after they have been granted approved person status by the FCA.

The Financial Services Act 2012 ('the Act') amended the powers to regulate approved persons and set out how the powers may be exercised by the PRA and FCA. The main relevant aspect of change was the extension of the Statements of Principle in APER to a wider set of activities, and their application to people approved by either regulator, meaning that both regulators will have the ability to discipline certain categories of approved person.

The FCA may specify customer dealing functions for any type of firm, and only the FCA may specify SIFs (significant influence functions) for solo-regulated firms.

The Act also includes the following detailed provisions on approved persons:

- The FCA can specify functions that will involve the person performing them dealing with customers of a regulated firm, or the property of those customers.

- The statements of principle (contained in APER) can relate to the conduct expected of persons not just in relation to the controlled functions they perform, but also in relation to other functions they perform, where those functions relate to the firm for which they hold their approval for carrying on regulated activities.

Individuals carrying out SIFs tend, by the nature of their roles, to occupy relatively senior positions within firms. In many cases, particularly those of the most senior roles, such as the chief executive, this means they will inevitably have responsibilities for both conduct and prudential issues. Therefore, the Act takes account of this.

## 3.1 The FCA's Approach to Approved Persons and Specifying Controlled Functions

## Controlled Functions

The controlled functions are:

- **Governing function** – individuals responsible for directing the affairs of the business. If the business is a company, then they will be the directors of that company. If the business is a partnership, then they will be the partners.

   - The 'governing function' is a significant influence function (SIF), on the basis that persons fulfilling these roles exercise a significant influence over the conduct of a firm's affairs.

- **Customer function** – individuals who would be giving/providing advice on dealing, arranging deals and managing investments. The individuals will have contact with customers in fulfilling their role, examples of customer functions are an investment adviser, the customer trading function or the investment management function. Customer functions are not SIFs.

## 4. COCON (Conduct Rules)

The PCBS recommended that the regulators develop a new set of banking standard rules that drew the existing statements of principles in APER (Statements of Principle and Code of Practice for Approved Persons) but apply to a wider group of individuals. The FCA approach means that the Conduct Rules apply to all UK employees of relevant firms, except staff carrying out purely ancillary functions who perform a role that is not specific to the financial services business of the firm, while the PRA applies the Conduct Rules to individuals performing a Senior Management Function and individuals who fall within the PRA Certification Regime.

By extending the Conduct Rules to all levels within a firm, the FCA is trying to embed a grass roots understanding of what acceptable and unacceptable behaviour is.

Conduct Rules appear to be the FCA's key tool in reinforcing the importance of appropriate values and behaviours at the most junior levels of firms.

Refer to section 2.3 of this chapter for further details.

In 2023, the PRA levied a fine on an individual for a breach of the Senior Manager Conduct Rule 3 ( _'You must take reasonable steps to ensure that the business of the firm for which you are responsible complies with relevant requirements and standards of the regulatory system'_ ).

The PRA fined the ex-CIO (Chief Information Officer) at TSB Bank plc, which was migrating its IT systems from a legacy system to a new platform. The failure led to customers not being able to access their accounts, payments from their accounts were not made (including payment for mortgages and loans), and it became headline news for the wrong reasons.

In 2022, TSB Bank Plc was fined jointly by the PRA (£18,900,000) and the FCA (£29,750,000), with the individual being fined £81,620 in April 2023 by the PRA.

The individual was responsible for providing leadership and strategic direction to the IT function, ensuring that the IT function was suitably experienced and qualified to carry out its responsibilities, as well as identifying, assessing, managing and reporting risks associated with the running of the IT function in line with the banks risk appetite. The CIO was also responsible for the performance of its obligations under the PRA's Outsourcing Rules, and this responsibility was split with another senior manager. Under that split, the individual was responsible for the bank's key outsourcing relationships and accountable for the operational relationship with third parties in relation to IT.

The PRA noted that it expected the CIO to act reasonably in carrying out their role and responsibilities, particularly in relation to the identification and mitigation of risks from an IT perspective. Under the Senior Manager Conduct Rules, the PRA also required the individual to take reasonable steps to ensure that the business of the bank for which they were responsible complied with relevant regulatory requirements and standards.

The PRA expects persons performing a SMF to take reasonable steps to ensure that the business has operating procedures and systems which include well-defined steps for such compliance and for ensuring that the business is run prudently. This required the individual to take reasonable steps in relation to the identification and mitigation of risks relating to the readiness of the bank's contractors and subcontractors.

The PRA identified that the ex-CIO had breached Senior Manager Conduct Rule 2 as they had failed to take reasonable steps to ensure that the bank complied with the PRA's Outsourcing Rules in adequately managing and appropriately supervising its outsourcing arrangement. In particular, the CIO failed to obtain sufficient assurance from the outsource provider in relation to the readiness of the new platform.

## 5. High-Level Standards: Introduction

The high-level standards section of the FCA Handbook refers to the overarching standards applying to all authorised firms and approved persons. It contains the following source books:

- **PRIN** – Principles for Businesses – the fundamental obligations of all firms.

- **SYSC** – Senior Management Arrangements, Systems and Controls – the responsibilities of directors and senior management.

- **COCON** – Code of Conduct.

- **COND** – Threshold Conditions – minimum standards for authorisation.

- **APER** – Statements of Principle and Code of Practice for Approved Persons – the fundamental obligations of approved persons.

- **FIT** – the Fit and Proper Test for employees and Senior Personnel.

- **FINMAR** – Financial Stability and Market Confidence.

- **TC** – **Training and Competence** – requirements for staff.

- **GEN** – General Provisions.

- **FEES** – Fee provisions for funding the regulatory bodies.
